Construction & clear space

CLEAR SPACE = 0.5× MARK HEIGHT
ASAllSquared
1Clear spaceAlways leave at least 0.5× the mark's height of clear space on every side. Nothing — text, image, edge — enters this zone.
2The rotationThe mark sits at exactly −2°. Never straighten it. Never increase the angle. The shadow always falls bottom-right.
3The stampThe ² stamp orbits the upper-right corner with a small overhang. It is part of the mark — never crop, recolour or omit it.
4Minimum sizesMark alone: 24×24px digital, 8mm print. Horizontal lockup: 120px wide digital, 32mm print. Below this, use the mark only.

The palette02

Cream paper. Ink rule. Lime stamp.

Three colours do most of the work — paper, ink, lime. Tomato is the single hot accent for the stamp, the warning, the final CTA. Sky, grape and rose are quiet supporting players for category and rhythm. Don't introduce new hues.

The type03

Three faces. No exceptions.

Archivo Black for headlines, Space Grotesk for body, JetBrains Mono for numerals, IDs and meta. No script. No serif. No system fallbacks except as final-line backup. Ship the fonts; don't rely on the user's machine.

The voice04

Confident. Plain. Slightly British.

We talk to grown-up professionals about money. Plain English, short sentences, dry edge. Never breezy, never preachy, never AI-flavoured. If you'd be embarrassed to read it aloud to a 50-year-old fit-out contractor, rewrite it.

Four voice pillars

Plain

Boring words for serious things. "Funds release on approval" — not "frictionless capital movement."

Direct

Short sentences. Active verbs. We say what we do and what we don't. We never bury the answer.

Dry

One eyebrow always slightly raised. We're entitled to an opinion about late payment because we built a business around fixing it.

Specific

Real numbers, real deals, real timestamps. £88,000. 72 hours. 04-Apr 18:42. Never "fast" — say how fast.

Do & don't

Write this

  • "Funds release on approval — typically the same day."
  • "FCA-authorised escrow. Ring-fenced. Yours either way."
  • "You did the work. You sent the invoice. The chase shouldn't be the job."
  • "Most disputes resolve at the evidence stage — because the evidence exists."
  • "One platform fee. Per deal. No cut of your future work."

Not this

  • "Revolutionising the way Britain pays." (Empty.)
  • "Empower your business with frictionless cashflow." (Slop.)
  • "Welcome to the future of work!" (Wrong audience.)
  • "Our AI-powered platform leverages…" (Stop.)
  • "Lightning-fast." (Say the actual time.)
